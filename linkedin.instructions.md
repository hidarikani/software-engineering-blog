# LinkedIn Post Instructions

## My Background
- I am a software engineer
- English is not my first language — my natural writing style is simpler and more direct than standard formal English
- Always infer my tone and style from how I write in the chat and preserve it in the final post

## Process
- I will share my ideas one by one in no particular order
- I will let you know when I am done sharing ideas
- Only then should you compile the final post
- Remove repeated thoughts and keep the post concise
- Before finalizing, fact-check the post and present findings as a numbered list so I can decide which points to apply

## Voice & Tone
- Keep the tone suitable for LinkedIn but preserve my natural, simple way of speaking
- Do not over-polish or make it sound too formal
- Write in first person
- Opinions and personal experiences should be clearly framed as such — not as absolute truths
- Use phrases like "in my view", "from what I've read", "at least for me", "I've come to believe" where appropriate

## Formatting
- LinkedIn does not support headings, so use emojis as visual paragraph/section dividers instead
- Use emojis to signal new thoughts or topic shifts, not just for decoration
- Avoid overly provocative or clickbait-style opening sentences — I prefer objective and nuanced over maximum views
- Bold important phrases or tool names where relevant
- Links should be inline with descriptive anchor text

## Content Guidelines
- Avoid naming specific vendors or platforms unless strictly necessary — keep things general to avoid offending anyone
- Balance criticism with acknowledgment of practical value where relevant
- Personal, concrete examples and specific recommendations are encouraged — they make the post more trustworthy
- If I mention specific apps, tools or plugins, look them up online and include direct links (e.g. Google Play Store, Chrome Web Store)

## File Naming
- When saving as markdown, use a short 3-4 word filename in kebab-case that describes the content (e.g. `fighting-the-feed.md`)
