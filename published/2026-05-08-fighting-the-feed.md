Social media has more in common with gambling than most people realize. And I say this as a software engineer.

It is kind of a public secret by now — social media platforms have borrowed mechanics from gambling to keep us engaged. When I started thinking about it more carefully, I realized it comes down to two things.

🎰 First — it is frictionless. The app is one tap away from your home screen. There is basically no barrier between you and the feed.

🎰 Second — the feed is algorithmically curated to serve unpredictable, variable content. Every time you open it, you see something slightly different. I don't think this is accidental — in my view, it is very much by design. Just like a slot machine, this unpredictability creates a small excitement: _what will I see this time?_ And that keeps you coming back.

🧠 Now, you might think — just use some willpower. But I've come to believe willpower is the wrong frame here. The urge to open the app is largely subconscious. It gets you when you are not thinking deeply — in between tasks, when you are commuting, when you are tired. From what I've read and heard, even the engineers and UX designers who built these systems are not immune to them.

💻 As someone who works with computers all day, I noticed this was quietly hurting my focus and performance. So I started doing something about it.

⚙️ The key insight for me was: you cannot fight this in the moment. But when you are rested and thinking clearly, you can set up things that protect your future self. Fight technology with technology.

⚠️ Completely blocking social media does not really work, at least for me. These platforms do have real value: staying in touch with friends, connecting with your local community. The problem is specifically the feed. You open the app to message someone, and somehow end up watching short videos for 20 minutes you never planned to watch.

🎯 So the goal is not to quit. It is to use it on your terms. Here is what actually worked for me:

📱 **On my phone** — I use an app called **Stay Focused** ([Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.stayfocused)). It lets you restrict when certain apps can be opened. My setting: social media is only available from 7PM to 9PM.

💻 **On my computer** — I use a Chrome plugin called **News Feed Eradicator** ([Chrome Web Store](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/news-feed-eradicator/fjcldmjmjhkklehbacihaiopjklihlgg)). It removes the feed entirely but keeps the rest of the website working fine. You can still use all the features — you just don't get pulled into the scroll.
